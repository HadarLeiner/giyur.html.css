Does the process takes less time in case someone already has a huge knowledge in judaism? In generally the time span won't differ so much because the Beis Din
wants to see that you have been learning enough time under guidance (not on your own ,which could lead to misunderstandings).
Another important question: where is the process easier ,in Israel or anywhere else ? First of all it pretty much depends if you have the right to make Aliyah.
If at least on of your grandparents is jewish you have the right to make Aliyah and for an Israeli citizen it's pretty easy to make a quick and easy Giur.
If you don't havd any jewish heritage ,then there would be another important factor:the country you live.
People from poorer countries like India,Mexico, African countries,Eastern Europe stand under the doubt of making giur for financial reasons
,which makes it pretty hard for them to get a Visa.
In most western countries with jewish communities the whole process will take about 2~3 years, while in Israel it could be done within a year!
(Very important though you need a letter from a Rabbi in your country which states that you have been in contact with the jewish community for at least a year).
So basically there is no definite answer ,rather depending on the individual situation of the situation.
So where is your role in the process? Basically we learn with you during the course of a year in order to prepare you for the Beis Din,
help you to make the application for the Beis Din, and will accompany you to the Beis Din.
We work together with the Beis Din of Harav Karelitz Sz"l in Bnei Brak where you have to appear for at least one time.
